Two lines across common two distinct points are equivalent

If

$$ \begin{array}{llll} A_1&\ni& a_0,&a_1,&a_2,&\dots\\ \\ A_2&\ni& a_0,&a_1,&a_3,&\dots \end{array}$$

are two lines, then

$$ \begin{array}{lllllll} A_1&=A_2\\ \\ A_1&=A_2&\ni& a_0,&a_1,&a_2,&a_3,&\dots \end{array}$$

Expand the definition of "point" into complex numbers on a complex plane

If \(z_0, z_1, z_2\) are three collinear points in a complex plane

$$ \begin{array}{llll} L_1&\ni& z_0,&z_1,&z_2,&\dots\\ \\ \end{array}$$

then

$$ \begin{array}{llll} L_1 +c&\ni& z_0+c,&z_1+c,&z_2+c,&\dots\\ \\ \end{array}$$

also collinear

$$ \begin{array}{llll} L_1 \cdot c&\ni& z_0\cdot c,&z_1\cdot c,&z_2\cdot c,&\dots\\ \\ \end{array}$$

also collinear

if \(z^*\) is a complex conjugate of \(z\), then

$$ \begin{array}{llll} {L_1}^*&\ni& {z_0}^*,&{z_1}^*,&{z_2}^*,&\dots\\ \\ \end{array}$$

also collinear
