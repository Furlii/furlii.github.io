Complex Functions Intro | Complex Numbers

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\begin{array}{lll} \text{Re}(z)=\dfrac{1}{2}\Big(z^*+z\Big)& \text{Im}(z)=\dfrac{i}{2}\Big(z^*-z\Big)\\ \\ \text{mgn}(z)=\sqrt{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}}z\cdot z^*}& \text{sgn}(z)=\dfrac{z}{\sqrt{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}}z\cdot z^*}}\\ \\ \text{arg}(z)=-i\cdot \ln \Big(\text{sgn}\;{z}\Big)& \\ \\ \end{array}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

the complex conjugate \(z^*\) reflects \(z\) over real axis

$$\phantom{|}$$

\(\text{Re}(z)\) function returns shadow of \(z\) projected on the real axis \(\mathbb{R}\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

\(\text{Im}(z)\) function returns the distance of \(z\) to \(\text{Re}(z)\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

magnitude function \(\text{mgn}(z)\) (commonly written as \(|z|\)) returns the distance of \(z\) to \(0+0i\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

signum function \(\text{sgn}(z)\) returns the shadow of \(z\) projected on the unit circle \(\mathbb{S}^1\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

argument function \(\text{arg}(z)\) main idea is to return angle formed by three points \(z , 0+0i , 1+0i\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

but due to nature of periodicity, argument function returns multiple values of \(\varphi+2k\pi\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

\(\varphi\) being angle of \(z , 0+0i , 1+0i\) while \(k\in\mathbb{Z}\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

Isometry Transformations : preserve distances

$$\phantom{|}$$

(euclidean) distance \(d\) between two points \(a_0\) and \(a_1\) in complex plane

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$d\overline{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}} a_0 , a_1}=(a_1-a_0)(a_1-a_0)^*$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

so for any isometry function \(f(z)\) and two arbitrary complex number \(a_0 , a_1\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$d\overline{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}} a_0 , a_1}=d\overline{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}} f(a_0) , f(a_1)}$$

Möbius Transformations : transforms lines and circles to lines or circles

$$\phantom{|}$$

has a general form

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=\dfrac{az+b}{cz+d}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

\(\forall a,b,c,d\in\mathbb{C}\) while \(ad-bc\neq 0\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

Conformal Transformations : preserves angles

$$\phantom{|}$$

all möbius transformations are conformal but not all conformal transformations are möbius

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=z^*$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=z+c$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

Geometric Transformations 2 | Complex Geometry

$$\phantom{|}$$

Homothety \(f(z)\) is defined as multiplication by element of \(\mathbb{R}\). It scales \(z\) with center at \(0+0i\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=z\cdot r \quad,\quad r\in\mathbb{R}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

it is a conformal transformation

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=z\cdot a$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

Geometric Inversion \(f(z)\) returns inverse of \(z\) over unit circle without reflecting over real axis
