Lines and Circles in Complex Plane | Complex Geometry

$$\phantom{|}$$

Lines : \(z\) lies on a line across \(a_0 , a_1\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\forall z\in L\overline{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}} a_0 , a_1}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

if and only if

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$(z-a_0)(a_1-a_0)^*=(z-a_0)^*(a_1-a_0)$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$z(a_1-a_0)^*-z^*(a_1-a_0)=a_0a_1^*-a_0^*a_1$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

Circles : \(z\) lies on a circle centered at \(a_0\) across \(a_1\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\forall z\in C\overline{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}} a_0 , a_1}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

if and only if

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$(z-a_0)(z-a_0)^*=(a_1-a_0)(a_1-a_0)^*$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$zz^*-z a_0^*-a_0z^*=a_1 a_1^* -a_1 a_0^* -a_0 a_1^*$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

Isometry Transformations : preserve distances

$$\phantom{|}$$

(euclidean) distance \(d\) between two points \(a_0\) and \(a_1\) in complex plane

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$d\overline{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}} a_0 , a_1}=(a_1-a_0)(a_1-a_0)^*$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

so for any isometry function \(f(z)\) and two arbitrary complex number \(a_0 , a_1\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$d\overline{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}} a_0 , a_1}=d\overline{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}} f(a_0) , f(a_1)}$$

Möbius Transformations : transforms lines and circles to lines or circles

$$\phantom{|}$$

has a general form

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=\dfrac{az+b}{cz+d}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

\(\forall a,b,c,d\in\mathbb{C}\) while \(ad-bc\neq 0\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

Conformal Transformations : preserves angles

$$\phantom{|}$$

all möbius transformations are conformal but not all conformal transformations are möbius

$$\phantom{|}$$

Polar Form | Complex Geometry

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\text{Re}(z)=\dfrac{1}{2}\Big(z^*+z\Big)$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\text{Im}(z)=\dfrac{i}{2}\Big(z^*-z\Big)$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\text{mgn}(z)=\sqrt{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}}z\cdot z^*}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\text{sgn}(z)=\dfrac{z}{\sqrt{\phantom{\overset{0}{|}}z\cdot z^*}}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\text{arg}(z)=-i\cdot \ln \Big(\text{sgn}{z}\Big)$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

the complex conjugate \(z^*\) reflects \(z\) over real axis

$$\phantom{|}$$

\(\text{Re}(z)\) function returns shadow of \(z\) projected on the real axis \(\mathbb{R}\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

\(\text{Im}(z)\) function returns the distance of \(z\) to \(\text{Re}(z)\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

magnitude function \(\text{mgn}(z)\) (commonly written as \(|z|\)) returns the distance of \(z\) to \(0+0i\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

signum function \(\text{sgn}(z)\) returns the shadow of \(z\) projected on the unit circle \(\mathbb{S}^1\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

argument function \(\text{arg}(z)\) main idea is to return angle formed by three points \(z , 0+0i , 1+0i\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

but due to nature of periodicity, argument function returns multiple values of \(\varphi+2k\pi\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

\(\varphi\) being angle of \(z , 0+0i , 1+0i\) while \(k\in\mathbb{Z}\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

values returned by \(\text{Re}(z)\) and \(\text{Im}(z)\) lies on real axis

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\text{Re}(z) , \text{Im}(z) \in \mathbb{R}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

values returned by \(\text{mgn}(z)\) also lies on real axis, particularly positive real axis

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\text{mgn}(z) \in \mathbb{R}^+$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

values returned by \(\text{sgn}(z)\) lies on circle group

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$\text{sgn}(z) \in \mathbb{S}^1$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

let \(\varphi_0\) and \(\varphi_1\) be two possible values of \(\text{arg}(z)\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

the value of $$\dfrac{1}{2\pi}\Big(\varphi_1-\varphi_0\Big) \in \mathbb{Z}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

any complex number \(z\) can be split into polar form

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$z=\text{mgn}(z) \cdot \text{sgn}(z)$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

or rectangular form

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$z=\text{Re}(z) + i \text{Im}(z)$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

Geometric Transformations | Complex Geometry

$$\phantom{|}$$

isometry transformations

$$\phantom{|}$$

Conjugation · Translation/Addition · Rotation · Homothety · Multiplication · Linear · Inversion · Division · Rational/Möbius

$$\phantom{|}$$

Conjugation \(f(z)\) reflects complex number \(z\) over real axis \(\mathbb{R}\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=z^*$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

it is an isometry transformation

$$\phantom{|}$$

Translation/Addition \(f(z)\) moves complex number \(z\) by \(c\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=z+c$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

it is an isometry transformation

$$\phantom{|}$$

Rotation \(f(z)\) is defined as multiplication by element of \(\mathbb{S}^1\). It rotates \(z\) with center at \(0+0i\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=z\cdot s \quad,\quad s\in\mathbb{S}^1$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

it is an isometry transformation

$$\phantom{|}$$

Homothety \(f(z)\) is defined as multiplication by element of \(\mathbb{R}\). It scales \(z\) with center at \(0+0i\)

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=z\cdot r \quad,\quad r\in\mathbb{R}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

it is a conformal transformation

$$\phantom{|}$$

Multiplication \(f(z)\) is a combination of rotation and homothety

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=z\cdot a$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

it is a conformal transformation

$$\phantom{|}$$

Complex Inversion \(f(z)\) returns inverse of \(z\) over unit circle combined with reflection over real axis

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=\dfrac{1}{z}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

it is a conformal transformation

$$\phantom{|}$$

Geometric Inversion \(f(z)\) returns inverse of \(z\) over unit circle without reflecting over real axis

$$\phantom{|}$$

$$f(z)=\Big(\dfrac{1}{z}\Big)^*=\dfrac{1}{z^*}$$

$$\phantom{|}$$

it is a conformal transformation

$$\phantom{|}$$
